The **Chinese Skin Microbiome Project (CSMP，中国人皮肤微生物组计划)** is a large-scale national initiative
aimed at systematically characterizing the skin microbiome of the Chinese population.
The project was conducted at **BGI-Research Shenzhen**, under the supervision of **Researcher Liang Xiao**.

## My Role

As a **core member** during my master's studies, I was primarily responsible for:

- **Bioinformatics analysis** — processing and interpreting large-scale metagenomic sequencing data from skin samples across diverse Chinese cohorts
- **Algorithm development** — designing and implementing bioinformatics pipelines and statistical methods tailored for skin microbiome profiling
- **Data integration** — integrating multi-site, multi-cohort skin microbiome datasets to enable population-level insights

## Project Highlights

- Focuses on the **Chinese population**, filling a critical gap in global skin microbiome research dominated by Western cohorts
- Employs **shotgun metagenomics** to achieve species- and strain-level resolution of skin microbial communities
- Investigates associations between the skin microbiome and factors such as **body site, age, gender, geography, and skin phenotypes**
- Provides a foundational reference dataset for future studies on skin health, dermatological diseases, and microbiome-targeted therapies

## Significance

The skin is the largest human organ and harbors a highly diverse and site-specific microbial ecosystem.
Establishing a comprehensive Chinese skin microbiome reference atlas is essential for understanding
population-specific microbiome features and their implications for skin health and disease.
CSMP represents a key step toward precision microbiome medicine in the Chinese clinical context.
